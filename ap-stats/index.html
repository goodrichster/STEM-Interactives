Understanding AP Statistics

AP Statistics introduces students to the major concepts and tools for collecting, analyzing, and drawing conclusions from data. The course covers nine units that align with the typical two-semester college introductory statistics course.

Exploring Data focuses on analyzing distributions of univariate data, including graphical and numerical techniques for describing patterns and departures from patterns.

Modeling Distributions introduces normal distributions, z-scores, and density curves as tools for understanding data patterns.

Correlation and Regression examines relationships between two variables using scatterplots, correlation, and least squares regression.

Collecting Data covers sampling methods, experimental design, and the importance of proper data collection techniques.

Probability provides the foundation for inferential statistics through the study of random variables and probability rules.

Random Variables explores discrete and continuous random variables and their probability distributions.

Sampling Distributions introduces the logic of statistical inference through the study of sampling distributions.

Confidence Intervals teaches how to estimate population parameters with a measure of uncertainty.

Significance Tests covers hypothesis testing and the interpretation of p-values in making statistical decisions.
